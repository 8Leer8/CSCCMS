Recent Awards and Recognition

Celebrating the Excellence of the College of Computing Studies

We take pride in acknowledging the achievements and milestones of our college. These recognitions highlight the commitment to excellence, innovation, and the collective efforts of our community in shaping the future of computing and technology.

Successful Deployment of VAWC Insights Portal by WMSU CCS at DILG IX

The WMSU College of Computing Studies (CCS) deployed the "VAWC Insights: Barangay VAWC Desk Portal" at DILG IX in Pagadian City on December 2, 2024. This project, in partnership with DILG IX, aims to improve the tracking and reporting of Violence Against Women and Children (VAWC) cases. The training introduced the portal's features for case documentation and data management, with feedback collected for future improvements. This initiative reflects WMSU’s commitment to community-driven innovation and safer, inclusive communities.

Parent-Teacher Assembly Strengthens Connection at WMSU College of Computing Studies

The WMSU College of Computing Studies held its annual Parent-Teacher Assembly on September 28, 2024, at the College of Medicine Amphitheater. The event introduced faculty members and highlighted key academic components such as the capstone project, internship programs, and student affairs. Discussions also emphasized the college’s commitment to student welfare and fostering a supportive community. The assembly reinforced the importance of open communication between students, parents, and faculty to ensure academic and personal success.

CCS Shines at WMSU COPC Awards Gala Night

At the WMSU COPC Awards Gala Night on September 4, 2024, the College of Computing Studies (CCS) was honored with a Plaque of Recognition for achieving 100% COPC compliance in both undergraduate and graduate programs. CCS also received a Plaque of Commendation for full compliance across all programs. This recognition reflects the college's commitment to academic excellence. CCS expressed gratitude to WMSU President Dr. Ma Carla Altea Ochotorena for her continued support and leadership.
