About the College of Computing Studies Student Council

The Student Council is the official representative body of the College of Computing Studies. It serves as a vital link between students, faculty, and administration, advocating for student concerns, addressing their needs, and fostering a supportive academic environment. Committed to student empowerment, the council organizes initiatives, events, and programs that enhance academic growth, leadership development, and community engagement. Through collaboration and active representation, it strives to create a dynamic and inclusive college experience, ensuring that every student's voice is heard and valued.

Empowering Leadership & Innovation

Dedicated to fostering a dynamic and inclusive academic environment, the council organizes initiatives that promote leadership, innovation, and professional growth. Through various programs and events, it enhances student engagement, strengthens the computing community, and contributes to the continuous improvement of the academic experience.

Enhancing Student Advocacy & Development

With a strong emphasis on advocacy, collaboration, and student development, the CCS Student Council serves as a bridge between students and administration. It ensures that student concerns are heard, promotes a culture of inclusivity, and helps shape a forward-thinking educational environment within the college.

CCS-CSC REGULAR SESSION

The CCS-CSC Executives held their 2nd regular session on February 27, 2025, at the CCS Organization's Office. The officers discussed upcoming projects for students, refining organizational policies and explored solutions to existing concerns, aiming to enhance student experience and organizational efficiency.

Advancing Student Interests and Organizational Excellence

The CCS-CSC is determined to uphold its mission of advocating the best interest of the student body and continuous improvement within the organization. With a shared vision for progress, CCS-CSC remains dedicated to making meaningful changes that will benefit the entire CCS student community.
